# Rainbow Rating — Research & Design (July 2026)

Research into existing LGBT+ certification/rating schemes and community-safety
evidence, feeding the design of Rainbow Navigator's six-colour community
rating. Full sources cited inline.

## Lessons from existing schemes

| Scheme | Model | Key weakness |
|---|---|---|
| Rainbow Tick (NZ) | Paid pass/fail employer certification, 5 areas | Benchmarks not public; not a true certification mark; community reputation "easy to get, tokenistic"; says little about customer experience |
| Pride Pledge (NZ) | Values pledge + subscription, self-assessed stocktake | Explicitly not an audit — pledge + payment buys visible collateral with no verification |
| HRC Corporate Equality Index (US) | 0-100 self-report survey; −25 point deduction for public anti-LGBTQ conduct | Self-reported, score inflation (13 perfect scores in 2002 → 765 in 2025); the deduction is its one anti-washing feature |
| Stonewall WEI (UK) | Employer self-assessment PLUS anonymous staff questionnaire | Employer-centric; but staff-voice-in-the-score is the right instinct |
| Safe Space Alliance (NZ-origin) | Free self-nominated "safe space" sticker | No criteria, no audit, no removal — the cautionary tale |
| Everywhere Is Queer | Queer-owned map, owner attestation + light approval | Honour-system ownership — but correctly treats ownership as a flag, not a score |
| Google Maps / Yelp attributes | Business self-toggles "LGBTQ+ friendly" | Zero verification; also exposes businesses to review-bombing |
| misterb&b / IGLTA (travel) | Post-stay 7-question guest survey → algorithmic threshold | Closest model to ours: the people affected are the raters |

Cross-cutting: wash-resistant schemes combine (1) the affected people as raters,
(2) multiple specific dimensions, (3) a deduction/removal mechanism. Vulnerable
schemes are binary, self-designated, or pay-to-display.

## What matters most to community members (ranked)

Sources: Counting Ourselves 2018/2022 (NZ trans survey), Stats NZ wellbeing
data, Youth19, venue-safety research.

1. Freedom from harassment (25% of NZ trans people faced public discrimination; 40% felt unsafe on public transport)
2. Staff behaviour — names, pronouns, respect (36% avoided doctors for fear of disrespect)
3. Bathrooms/changing rooms (~49% of trans men often/always avoided public bathrooms; 58% avoided gyms/pools)
4. Ability to be visibly yourself (hold hands, dress authentically)
5. Visible year-round welcome signals (June-only rainbows are the #1 washing red flag)
6. Inclusive forms/language/systems
7. Intersectional inclusion — takatāpui, Pasifika/MVPFAFF+, Asian, disabled rainbow people
8. Genuine year-round community commitment

## The design: six colours, one dimension each

Colour meanings follow the traditional flag (red=life, orange=healing,
yellow=sunlight, green=nature, blue=serenity, purple=spirit).

| Colour | Dimension | Post-visit question (1-5) |
|---|---|---|
| 🟥 Red — Safety | Freedom from harassment | "Did you feel safe here — free from hostility, harassment or being made unwelcome?" |
| 🟧 Orange — Staff | Staff behaviour & competence | "Did staff get it right — your name, pronouns, and respectful, unawkward service?" |
| 🟨 Yellow — Visibility | Visible year-round welcome | "Could you tell you'd be welcome before you even walked in — and not just during Pride month?" |
| 🟩 Green — Facilities | Trans-inclusive facilities & systems | "Did the practical stuff fit you — toilets, changing rooms, forms and sign-ins that don't force you into a box?" |
| 🟦 Blue — Belonging | Freedom to be yourself | "Could you fully be yourself here — hold hands, dress how you dress, show up as you are?" |
| 🟪 Purple — Community spirit | Give-back & whole-rainbow inclusion | "Does this place genuinely show up for the community — including takatāpui, Pasifika, disabled and trans whānau — beyond the flag?" |

## Anti-rainbow-washing rules

1. Ownership is a verified flag, never a votable score (owner attestation +
   documentation or two community vouches; revocable).
2. The business can never light its own rainbow — segments fill only from
   community post-visit ratings; business self-description displays separately.
3. A complete rainbow requires ALL six segments ≥ threshold (e.g. 4.0) with
   minimum review count and recency (e.g. ≥5 reviews in 18 months,
   time-decayed). One dimension cannot compensate for another.
4. Incident deductions: a moderated incident report can grey out the red
   segment pending resolution regardless of averages (the CEI −25 lesson).
5. Purple asks for evidence: optional structured tags ("hosted community
   event", "sponsors rainbow org", "te reo / takatāpui-inclusive",
   "accessible venue"); yellow prompts "visited outside Pride month?".
6. Guard the raters: light account verification, per-venue rate limits, and
   brigading monitoring to protect rainbow-owned venues from hostile
   review-bombing.
